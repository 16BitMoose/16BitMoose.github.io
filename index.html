Habitat

Moose inhabit diverse ecosystems, including boreal and deciduous forests, as well as marshes and meadows. They thrive in regions with ample vegetation for browsing and are commonly found in North America, Europe, and Asia.

Anatomy

Moose, the giants of the deer family, typically stand 1.8 to 2.1 meters at the shoulder. Adult males weigh between 540 to 730 kilograms, while females are somewhat smaller. Their colossal size, accentuated by impressive antlers in males, makes them a commanding presence in the diverse landscapes they inhabit, marking them as true giants of the wild.

Diet

Moose sustain themselves with a diverse herbivorous diet, primarily consisting of aquatic plants, shrubs, and trees. Their selective browsing includes willow, birch, and aquatic vegetation. While predominantly herbivores, moose occasionally consume aquatic vegetation, showcasing their adaptability in sourcing sustenance from the ecosystems they inhabit.
